## The Premise — Construction, meet Design

Design is bound by means and methods. They've remained largely unchanged for 70 years... until now. A printer lays structural concrete exactly where the design intends it, one bead at a time. Curves cost what straight lines cost. Formwork disappears. The wall you imagined is the wall you get.

Formless concrete + perfect translation = design intent.

## Technology

3D concrete printing, 3DCP, is construction by addition. A machine extrudes an engineered mortar in precise layers, straight from the design file. Material goes only where the structure needs it. No forms, no offcuts, no waiting on framing crews.

1. The printer — Gantry systems or robotic arms at millimeter repeatability.
2. The material — Printable mortars engineered to hold shape and cure strong.
3. The print — Walls rise in continuous beads. Openings print in place.

## Why It Matters

- **Waste** — Additive manufacturing uses only what it needs.
- **Speed** — Wall systems print in days with a crew of two to three.
- **Future proof** — Monolithic concrete: hurricane resistance, termite immunity, fire performance.
- **Design freedom** — Curves and organic forms at the same cost as straight lines.
