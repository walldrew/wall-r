Services

Three ways Wallr works

Consulting to shorten your learning curve, distribution to put the right machine in your hands, construction to print the walls themselves.

1Consulting

I will curtail your learning curve about 3DCP drastically. I've gone through the frustration that can only be learned in the field. I cut my teeth on concrete dust and I'm eager to share my lessons to save you time, money, and frustration.

I educate as well as evaluate your needs and goals, recommend the best solution, and assist you with the path to procurement.

2Distribution

As an authorized vendor for several different technologies, I've built partnerships with manufacturers to give you a wide array of options, along with follow-on support.

I will guide you from purchase to print and train your team to become proficient operators: machine selection, material supply chain, site setup, and operator certification.

3Construction

I provide 3DCP services that can include structures such as residential homes, architectural moments, light commercial space, landscape design elements, and more.

I partner with local GCs to offer subcontracting and entire build services, with the wall package delivered as a defined, insured scope.

How an engagement runs

Simple, in four steps

Intro call

Thirty minutes. You describe the project or the ambition; I tell you honestly whether 3DCP fits.

Assessment

Site, design, and goals reviewed against the printable envelope and your budget reality.

Proposal

A written scope with real numbers: consulting plan, machine package, or wall package quote.

Delivery

Advice in the room, a machine in your yard, or walls on your slab. Field-tested, either way.

Questions

Before you ask

Which printer brands do you carry?

Several, on purpose. The right machine depends on your projects, site types, and team. The assessment matches the technology to the mission instead of forcing one brand on every problem.

Do you train our operators?

Yes. Distribution includes training from purchase to print: machine operation, mix handling, print planning, and the field habits that keep a print day boring, which is the goal.

Can you just look at our plans and tell us if they print?

Yes. A plans review against the printable envelope is one of the most useful small engagements there is, and it usually pays for itself in avoided redesign.

One conversation. Field-tested answers, not brochure answers.
