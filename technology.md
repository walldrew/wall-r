# Technology — Additive Construction Manufacturing

3D concrete printing, 3DCP, is construction by addition. A gantry or robotic arm extrudes an engineered mortar in precise layers, straight from the design file.

## How It Works

1. **The printer** — Gantry systems that span the slab, or robotic arms that reach around it. Millimeter repeatability.
2. **The material** — Printable mortars engineered to hold shape wet and cure strong.
3. **The toolpath** — Architecture becomes a machine path. The design file sliced into layers, beads, and speeds.
4. **The print** — Walls rise in continuous beads. Openings, chases, and conduit paths print in place.
5. **The finish** — Printed shells integrate with conventional trades: roofing, MEP, windows, insulation.
6. **The proof** — Permitted, engineered, and insured projects across Florida and the US.

## How a Print Actually Runs

1. Model and engineer — Design developed against the printable envelope. Licensed engineer letters the wall system.
2. Slice and stage — Model becomes toolpath. Machine set to slab, pump charged, mix dialed.
3. Print — Walls rise in continuous passes. Days, not weeks, from slab to top of wall.
4. Integrate — Bond beams, reinforcement, and embeds go in. Conventional trades take over.

## Comparison

| | 3DCP | Wood frame | Concrete block |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wall schedule | Days, crew of 2–3 | Weeks, full framing crew | Weeks, masonry crew |
| Design freedom | Curves at no premium | Rectilinear economics | Rectilinear economics |
| Waste stream | Material only where needed | Offcuts and packaging | Cuts, mortar loss |
| Storm and fire | Monolithic concrete | Engineered to code | Strong, joint dependent |
| Labor exposure | Low, machine led | High, market dependent | High, skilled trade |

## Glossary

- **Bead** — One extruded pass of material. Beads stack into layers; layers become the wall.
- **Toolpath** — The machine's route, generated from the model.
- **Gantry vs arm** — A gantry frames the whole slab; a robotic arm reaches from a base.
- **Cold joint** — Where fresh material meets cured material. Managed by print planning and mix design.
- **Bond beam** — The reinforced tie at the top of a wall.
- **Printable envelope** — The set of shapes a given machine and mix can produce reliably.
