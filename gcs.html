Subcontract the shell

Wallr prints the wall package as a defined scope: engineered, insured, and sequenced into your critical path. A print crew of two to three replaces weeks of framing labor exposure, and the corduroy shell hands off cleanly to your trades.

De-risk your first print

First 3DCP job? I've run the floor on the biggest one there is. From permitting conversations and engineering letters to inspector walkthroughs and pour day logistics, you get field-tested answers, not brochure answers.

What the scope includes

Machine mobilization, printable material, print crew, embeds and bond beam coordination, and QC documentation. Slab, roof, MEP, openings, and finishes stay with your trades, exactly where they belong.

Schedule integration

Walls print in days once the slab cures. We sequence around your inspections and keep the site usable: one machine footprint, no formwork laydown, no framing package on the ground.

Questions

What GC's ask first

Who carries the engineering?

The printed wall system is delivered under an engineered design by a licensed engineer, coordinated with your engineer of record. You get stamped drawings, not promises.

What about insurance and warranty?

The wall scope is delivered as an insured subcontract with QC documentation per lift. Specifics are spelled out in the subcontract, in writing, before mobilization.

What does my crew need to do differently?

Very little. Keep the slab clear during print days, then treat the shell like a very straight, very solid wall: your openings, roof, and trades proceed as planned.
